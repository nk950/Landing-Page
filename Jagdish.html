Jagdish Chandra Bose

Jagdish Chandra Bose was born on November 30, 1858, in Mymensingh (now in Bangladesh). He was a visionary scientist whose contributions to the field of biophysics have had a long-lasting effect on science. Bose’s early interest in the natural world led him to pursue studies in biology, chemistry, and physics.

One of Bose’s significant achievements was the invention of the Crescograph, a device that could measure plant growth and response to external stimuli. Through his experiments, he demonstrated that plants have a sensitive and dynamic nature, responding to various factors like light, temperature, and chemicals. This invention laid the foundation for the field of plant biology and contributed to our understanding of the interconnectedness of all living things.

Bose’s contributions extended beyond the laboratory. He was a strong advocate for scientific research in India and worked towards building a scientific temper in society. Despite facing challenges during the colonial era, Bose’s dedication to science led him to establish the Bose Institute in Kolkata, which became a hub for interdisciplinary research.

Along with his research efforts, Jagdish Chandra Bose was a pioneer in the field of wireless communication. He demonstrated the feasibility of using radio waves for communication. Bose’s legacy is all about believing in the democratization of knowledge, making efforts to bring science to the masses and inspiring future generations of scientists.

Today, he is remembered as a visionary scientist who bridged the gap between traditional knowledge and modern science, contributing to India’s progress on the global scientific stage.
