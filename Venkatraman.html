Venkatraman Ramakrishnan

Venkatraman Ramakrishnan, a scientist and Nobel Laureate in Chemistry, was born on April 6, 1952, in Chidambaram, Tamil Nadu, India. His journey from a small town in India to the pinnacle of scientific achievement is inspiring.

Ramakrishnan’s early education in India laid the foundation for his scientific curiosity. He later pursued higher studies in the United States, earning his Ph.D. in Physics. However, his true passion lay in biology, leading him to transition to structural biology—a field that explores the shapes and structures of biological molecules.

In 2009, Ramakrishnan, along with two other scientists, was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his groundbreaking work on the structure and function of the ribosome, a vital cellular component responsible for protein synthesis. His research provided unprecedented insights into the molecular machinery of life.

Ramakrishnan’s achievements extend beyond the laboratory. He has been an advocate for scientific research and education in India. In 2015, he assumed the presidency of the Royal Society, one of the oldest and most prestigious scientific societies in the world, becoming the first Indian-origin scientist to hold this position. He has been involved in initiatives to promote scientific research and collaboration between Indian and international institutions. His efforts inspire young Indian scientists and students to pursue excellence in the field of science.
